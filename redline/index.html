Why a goal, not just "improve"

"Make this clause better" has no single answer — a liability clause redrafted to protect the service provider looks nothing like one redrafted to be balanced, or one fixed purely for enforceability. By naming the goal first, the revision becomes intentional: every change serves a purpose you chose, and the tool explains how. It works on any clause from any agreement, and every output is flagged for a lawyer to confirm.

Decision-support, not legal advice. Redline Rationale suggests revisions and explains the reasoning to speed up drafting. Suggested wording must be reviewed by a qualified lawyer before use.
